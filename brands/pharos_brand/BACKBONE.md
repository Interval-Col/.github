# Pháros — Backbone
> Brand strategy and conceptual foundation for the **Pháros product family** — the software that replaces the COBOL LIS, born from LCH's Quality Control module.
> This document defines who Pháros *is* — its meaning, soul, and reason for existing — independent of how it looks or speaks.

---

## 1. Cuerpo · Body
> The container. What Pháros *is* in plain terms, before any emotion or interpretation.

Pháros is the software family that replaces the COBOL LIS across the holding's labs. It was born from one discipline inside Laboratorio Clínico Hematológico — **Quality Control**: the module that monitors, validates, and certifies the integrity of every clinical process that happens inside the lab — from sample handling to result delivery. That founding discipline still sets the family's tone.

It is built for the people who run the lab and the holding, not for patients. It speaks the language of internal operations, calibration, verification, and trust.

---

## 2. Alma · Soul
> The emotion the brand transmits when interacted with. The story it tells.

Pháros exists because the lab's promise to patients depends on something invisible: the quality of every step behind the result. A diagnosis is only as trustworthy as the system that produced it.

Pháros is the silent guardian of that trust. It does not perform tests; it makes sure tests are performed correctly. It does not generate diagnoses; it guarantees the conditions under which diagnoses can be trusted. It is the lighthouse on the rocky coast — not the ship, not the harbor, but the unblinking light that lets every ship come home safely.

The lab team interacts with Pháros daily. Pháros must feel like a calm, vigilant ally — never alarmist, never bureaucratic, always present. It catches errors before they become incidents. It speaks up when something drifts. It rests confidently when everything is in order.

If LCH's mantra is *"reading life in tissue, using technology to see the invisible,"* Pháros is the discipline that makes that reading honest. The light that reveals what hides — first within the lab itself, then within the patient.

The lighthouse names the whole family; its members each take their place in the same waters. Where Pháros guides from the coast, **Timón** — the ERP — takes the helm and steers the operation from inside the vessel: same sea, complementary roles.

## 3. Ser · Personality
> Archetypes — who Pháros is when it expresses itself.

### Sage (primary)
Pháros knows. It holds the knowledge of every protocol, every threshold, every acceptable variance. It draws from the accumulated wisdom of clinical practice and applies it without bias. Its authority comes from understanding, not from rules.

### Caregiver (secondary)
Pháros protects. It exists to keep the lab — and by extension, every patient — safe from invisible failures. Its vigilance is a form of love: quiet, constant, undramatic. It catches mistakes the way a careful nurse catches a falling tray.

### Inherited from LCH: Creator
Pháros is built on innovation. It uses modern tools to solve a problem that older labs solved with paperwork and hope. It is technology in service of life — exactly as LCH's own culture demands.

---

## 4. Propuesta de Valor · Value Proposition
> What Pháros offers that nothing else in its category does.

Pháros turns quality control from a compliance burden into a daily companion. Most QC systems are forms to fill out, rules to follow, audits to survive. Pháros is a presence that watches with you and quietly ensures the lab's promise to its patients is being kept — every shift, every sample, every result.

It is software with a soul that matches LCH's: *vidas, no clientes.*

---

## 5. Valores · Values
> The non-negotiable commitments that shape every decision.

### Veracidad — Truthfulness
Pháros never softens a finding. If a process is out of spec, Pháros says so plainly. If a result is uncertain, Pháros surfaces the uncertainty. It is incapable of polite lies because patients depend on what Pháros sees.

### Vigilancia serena — Calm vigilance
Pháros watches without alarm. It distinguishes between drift and disaster, between a minor variance worth noting and a critical issue that must stop the line. It earns trust by not crying wolf.

### Transparencia — Transparency
Pháros shows its work. Every flag has a reason, every threshold is traceable, every audit trail is readable. The lab team should never feel that Pháros is a black box — Pháros is a window.

### Inherited from LCH: Vidas, no clientes — Lives, not customers
Pháros exists for one ultimate reason: a real person is waiting for a real result. Every quality check is, at its root, an act of care for a human being.

## 6. Meet Your Fan
> Who Pháros is built to serve. Not casual users — devoted ones.

These three are the founding fans — the QC and LIS personas the family grew up serving. Each new surface (ERP, Admisiones, CRM) adds its own, but they all expect what these three do.

**The Lab Technologist on a long shift.** They don't have time for clunky software. They need Pháros to be quick, to be readable, to confirm what's working and surface what's not — without making them stop their flow. When Pháros works, they stop noticing it. That's the highest compliment.

**The QC Coordinator.** They are the human counterpart to Pháros. They review trends, sign off on drift reports, manage corrective actions. They need Pháros to be a partner: rigorous in detection, generous in context, ready with the data needed to make decisions.

**The Lab Director.** They sleep better because Pháros exists. They use Pháros to verify that the operation is running clean — not because they don't trust their team, but because trust requires verification, and verification requires a system as patient and unblinking as Pháros.

## 7. Talk to Me · Voice
> How Pháros speaks. The tone, the register, the linguistic fingerprint.

Pháros speaks in **Spanish primary, English secondary**, in a register that is **technically precise but humanly warm**. Never cold. Never bureaucratic.

- Uses simple, direct language. Avoids jargon when plain Spanish will do.
- Reports facts plainly: *"Tres muestras fuera de rango en el último turno"* — not *"Anomalía detectada en el subsistema de cuantificación."*
- Frames findings as observations, not accusations: *"El instrumento 3 está derivando hacia el límite superior"* — not *"Falla en instrumento 3."*
- Celebrates good states quietly: *"Todo en orden"*, *"Calibración estable"*. Never hype. Never emojis. Never exclamation marks.
- When something is wrong, urgency comes from clarity, not from volume: *"Detener la corrida. La control fall&oacute;."*
- Never apologetic. Pháros does its job; it doesn't need to say sorry for finding problems.

## 11. Receta Visual · Visual Recipe
> The ingredients of Pháros's visual world. Translated into design rules in `BRAND.md`.

- **Character**: lab professionals at work — focused, calm, real (not stock-photo)
- **Spatial**: clean lab interiors, natural light, minimal clutter — the calm of a library more than the bustle of a hospital
- **Colors**: the Pháros mark — burgundy + a single point of red (the pilot light). Each sub-brand adds one accent from the family palette; QC's burgundy/red lives on as the LIS state palette. Muted neutrals. Almost no other color — Pháros earns its restraint.
- **Elements**: clean grids, generous whitespace, precise typography, charts with strict alignment
- **Communication**: as if you could see the integrity of every process at a glance — clarity over decoration
